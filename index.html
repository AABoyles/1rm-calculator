What is a 1RM?

Your one-rep max (1RM) is the most weight you can lift for a single rep with good form. It's the standard measure of absolute strength for a given exercise.

You rarely need to actually attempt a true 1RM — grinding out a max single is taxing and carries injury risk. Instead, this calculator estimates it from a set you already did.

1 Pick the exercise — e.g. Back Squat
2 Enter the weight you lifted and how many reps you completed
3 Read your estimated 1RM — then hit % Table to see training weights at every intensity zone

For best accuracy, use a set where you reached close to failure — ideally between 3 and 10 reps. Very high-rep sets (15+) become less reliable because fatigue and technique break down before true muscular failure.

Marzagão Formula

This calculator uses the Marzagao weight-dependent formula, which adjusts the rep-to-1RM relationship based on how heavy the working weight is. Heavier loads yield a different fatigue curve than lighter loads, so a single fixed multiplier (like Epley or Brzycki) systematically over- or under-estimates at the extremes.
